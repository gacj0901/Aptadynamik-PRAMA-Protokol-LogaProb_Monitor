# Empirical Result 007 — Onset experiment separates contradictory tension from saturation truncation

A preregistered onset experiment over 50 neutral topics and 150 long trajectories shows that contradictory pressure produces sustained post-onset growth of long-memory PRAMA tension Ξ, while saturation pressure truncates the trajectory.



Contradictory onset passed for all three memory kernels:



- β = 0.3: mean DiD = +0.332974, 50/50 topics > 0

- β = 0.6: mean DiD = +0.197808, 50/50 topics > 0

- β = 0.9: mean DiD = +0.119346, 50/50 topics > 0



Saturation onset passed the survival endpoint:



- mean survival_saturation = 0.459632

- saturation survival < control survival in 50/50 topics

- saturation completed post-stage in 0/50 topics



Therefore, Ψ does not induce a homogeneous Ξ response. The form of pressure determines the dynamic regime:



- contradictory pressure produces accumulated internal tension;

- saturation pressure consumes margin and truncates the generative trajectory.
